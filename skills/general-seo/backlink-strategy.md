# Backlink Strategy — General SEO Skill

> Reusable link building methodology for any project. Zero project-specific references.

---

## 1. Link Building Philosophy

Links remain one of Google's strongest ranking signals. The goal is acquiring editorially-placed links from topically relevant, authoritative sites — not volume for volume's sake.

**Core principle:** Every link should be one that a human editor would place because the target page genuinely adds value for their audience.

---

## 2. Prospect Identification Methods

### 2.1 Competitor Backlink Analysis

1. Enter top 3-5 competitors into Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Ubersuggest
2. Pull their referring domains (filter DA > 25)
3. Identify domains linking to competitors but NOT to you
4. Score by: relevance to your niche, DA, traffic, editorial quality
5. Add qualified prospects to your outreach pipeline

### 2.2 Content-Based Prospecting

| Method | How It Works | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Resource page link building | Find pages that curate tools/resources in your niche, pitch your page as an addition | Category and tool pages |
| Broken link building | Find broken links on relevant sites, offer your content as a replacement | Any page with good content |
| Skyscraper technique | Find popular content in your niche, create something better, pitch to sites linking to the original | Blog posts, guides |
| Data-driven content | Create original research/data, pitch to journalists and bloggers | Homepage, blog posts |
| Expert roundups | Contribute expert quotes to roundup articles | Founder/brand building |

### 2.3 Relationship-Based Prospecting

| Method | How It Works | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Guest posting | Write original content for relevant publications | Brand awareness + links |
| HARO / Connectively / Qwoted | Respond to journalist queries with expert quotes | High-DA media links |
| Podcast appearances | Appear on niche podcasts (show notes link) | Brand + links |
| Industry events / webinars | Speak at events (event page links) | Authority building |
| Partnerships | Co-create content with complementary businesses | Mutual benefit links |

### 2.4 Digital PR

| Angle | Description | Target Publications |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Founder story | Company origin, mission, unique perspective | Startup media, business press |
| Data story | Original research, survey results, trend analysis | News outlets, industry publications |
| Contrarian take | Challenge industry assumptions with evidence | Opinion sections, thought leadership |
| Industry first | First to market, unique approach, new category | Tech press, niche media |
| Social impact | Community, sustainability, inclusion angle | General media, cause-related outlets |

## 3. Prospect Qualification Scoring

### Scoring Matrix (1-5 scale each)

| Factor | Weight | Score Criteria |
|--------|:---:|----------------|
| Domain Authority | 25% | 1: DA <20, 2: 20-35, 3: 35-50, 4: 50-70, 5: 70+ |
| Topical Relevance | 30% | 1: Unrelated, 2: Tangential, 3: Same industry, 4: Same niche, 5: Exact topic match |
| Monthly Traffic | 15% | 1: <1K, 2: 1-10K, 3: 10-50K, 4: 50-200K, 5: 200K+ |
| Editorial Standards | 15% | 1: Spammy, 2: Low quality, 3: Average, 4: Good editorial, 5: Premium publication |
| Link Placement | 15% | 1: Footer/sidebar, 2: Author bio, 3: Resource section, 4: In-content contextual, 5: Featured/highlighted |

**Minimum qualifying score:** 3.0 weighted average

### Disqualification Signals

- Site is clearly a link farm or PBN
- Excessive outbound links (>100 per page)
- No organic traffic (dead site)
- Unrelated niche with no editorial bridge
- Site has Google penalty indicators (sudden traffic drop)
- "Write for us" page with no editorial standards
- Pay-for-link pricing on the page

## 4. Anchor Text Strategy

### Target Distribution

| Anchor Type | Target % | Examples |
|-------------|:---:|---------|
| Branded | 30-40% | "CompanyName", "CompanyName.com" |
| Partial match | 20-25% | "best [keyword] tools", "[keyword] platform" |
| Exact match | 5-10% | "[exact primary keyword]" |
| Naked URL | 10-15% | "https://example.com/page" |
| Generic / contextual | 15-20% | "learn more", "this guide", "according to their research" |

### Rules

- NEVER exceed 15% exact match anchors — triggers over-optimization penalties
- Branded anchors should be the most common type
- Vary anchors across linking domains (don't use same anchor from every site)
- Let the linking site choose natural anchor text when possible
- For guest posts, use branded or partial match only
- Track anchor distribution monthly and rebalance if needed

## 5. Link Velocity Guidelines

### Safe Growth Rates

| Site Age | Safe Monthly New Referring Domains |
|----------|:---:|
| 0-6 months | 5-15 |
| 6-12 months | 10-30 |
| 1-2 years | 20-50 |
| 2+ years | 30-100+ |

### Red Flags

- Gaining 50+ referring domains in a month when you normally get 5
- All links acquired in a single week
- All links from the same country/language
- All links with the same anchor text
- Sudden stop after a burst (unnatural pattern)

**Principle:** Steady, consistent growth looks natural. Spikes followed by flatlines look manufactured.

## 6. Outreach Sequence Templates

### Initial Pitch (Day 0)

```
Subject: [Personalized hook related to their content]

Hi [Name],

I came across your [article/resource page] on [topic] — [specific compliment about their content].

I recently published [your content description] that [specific value proposition — what it adds that their audience would find useful].

Would you be open to taking a look? I think it could be a valuable addition to [specific section of their page].

[Your name]
[Your title / site]
```

### Follow-Up 1 (Day 3)

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Just wanted to bump this up — I know inboxes get busy.

[One-sentence reminder of the value proposition].

Happy to answer any questions or provide additional context.

[Your name]
```

### Follow-Up 2 (Day 7)

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Last follow-up from me — I don't want to be a pest!

[Reframe the value slightly — different angle or additional benefit].

Either way, keep up the great work on [their site/content].

[Your name]
```

### Break-Up (Day 14 — only if they've opened emails)

```
Subject: Closing the loop

Hi [Name],

I'll take the hint — looks like this isn't the right fit. No worries at all.

If you ever need [your expertise area] content or sources for future pieces, feel free to reach out.

[Your name]
```

### Email Personalization Rules

- **Always** reference a specific piece of their content (not generic)
- **Always** explain why your content adds value to their specific audience
- **Never** ask for a link directly in the first email (ask them to "take a look")
- **Never** send templated emails without personalization
- **Never** offer payment for links in the email
- Match their tone (formal publication = formal email, casual blog = casual email)

## 7. Toxic Link Identification

### Warning Signs

| Signal | Severity | Action |
|--------|:---:|--------|
| DA < 10 with no traffic | Low | Monitor |
| Site in unrelated foreign language | Medium | Disavow if anchor is spammy |
| Known PBN / link farm | High | Disavow immediately |
| Site-wide footer/sidebar link | Medium | Request removal or disavow |
| Exact-match anchor from low-quality site | High | Disavow |
| Sudden batch of links from same IP/network | High | Disavow the batch |
| Adult/gambling/pharma site linking to you | High | Disavow immediately |

### Disavow Process

1. Export all backlinks from Google Search Console
2. Identify toxic links using the signals above
3. Attempt manual removal (email webmaster) for highest-severity links
4. Create disavow file for links that can't be removed
5. Submit via Google Search Console Disavow Tool
6. Re-audit quarterly

## 8. Internal Link Building

Often overlooked but highly effective:

### Quick Wins

1. **New page published?** Add internal links from 3-5 existing related pages
2. **Blog post published?** Link to 1-2 commercial pages with keyword-rich anchors
3. **Page not ranking well?** Add internal links from higher-authority pages on your site
4. **Orphan pages?** Every page should have at least 3 internal links pointing to it

### Internal Link Audit (Monthly)

- Check for orphan pages (0 internal links)
- Check for pages with excessive internal links (>100)
- Verify anchor text relevance
- Ensure reciprocal hub-to-child linking

## 9. Link Building KPIs

| Metric | How to Track | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| New referring domains / month | Ahrefs, Search Console | Site-age-appropriate (see velocity table) |
| Average DA of new links | Ahrefs, Moz | > 30 |
| Anchor text distribution | Ahrefs, manual audit | Within target ratios |
| Outreach response rate | Email tracking | > 10% |
| Outreach conversion rate | Manual tracking | > 3% |
| Cost per link (if using paid outreach) | Budget / links acquired | < industry average for your niche |
| Referral traffic from links | GA4 referral report | Positive trend |

## 10. Link Building Campaign Checklist

Before launching any link building campaign:

- [ ] Target pages identified (which pages need links?)
- [ ] Target keywords confirmed (what are we trying to rank for?)
- [ ] Prospect list built (minimum 50 qualified prospects)
- [ ] Prospects scored and prioritized
- [ ] Outreach templates written and personalized per batch
- [ ] Anchor text strategy defined for this campaign
- [ ] Tracking spreadsheet set up (prospect, status, dates, outcome)
- [ ] Current backlink baseline captured (referring domains, DA distribution)
- [ ] Email sending tool configured (warm domain, SPF/DKIM, reasonable daily limits)
- [ ] Follow-up sequence scheduled

After campaign:

- [ ] Results documented (links acquired, DA, anchor text used)
- [ ] Anchor text distribution still within targets?
- [ ] Link velocity still within safe range?
- [ ] Referral traffic from new links visible in GA4?
- [ ] Ranking changes noted for target keywords?
- [ ] Lessons learned documented for next campaign

## 11. Link Building by Page Type

| Page Type | Best Link Strategies | Anchor Guidance |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------|
| Homepage | Digital PR, founder stories, brand mentions | Branded anchors (60%+) |
| Category pages | Resource page inclusion, niche directories, guest posts | Partial match (40%) + branded (40%) |
| Product pages | Product reviews, comparison articles, affiliate | Product name or branded |
| Blog posts | Skyscraper, data-driven, HARO, social sharing | Descriptive / contextual (50%+) |
| Comparison pages | "Best X" roundups, review sites | Partial match + branded |
| Tool / calculator pages | Resource pages, "useful tools" lists | Descriptive ("this calculator") |
