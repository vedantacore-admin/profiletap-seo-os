## Why Creators Need a Business Card That Works Offline

### Your link-in-bio cannot go to a meeting for you

Link-in-bio tools are excellent for your audience — the people who find you through your content and want a way to access everything you make. They are not built for brand relationships. A brand manager at a creator event does not want to follow your Instagram handle and find your link-in-bio. They want to receive your media kit, understand your reach, see your collaboration history, and have your contact details — in one interaction, in person, in under thirty seconds.

NFC sharing closes that gap. Your ProfileTap card carries everything a brand needs to begin a conversation: your profile, your media kit link, your contact details, and your analytics summary. One tap. Done.

### Paper cards undercut your brand

You produce high-quality video, photography, or written content. You have a visual identity. You have spent months or years building a brand that communicates professionalism and creativity. A paper business card — flat, static, and identical in format to every other professional's card — is a step backward from the standard you set with your content.

A ProfileTap NFC card that opens a visually rich, themed profile on a brand manager's phone is consistent with the quality of your work. It is the first impression that matches the impression you have already built online.

### No analytics on who engaged after the event

After a creator summit, you have had conversations with twenty-three brand managers, five fellow creators, two production company representatives, and a podcast host. You exchanged details with most of them. You have no idea who followed up by looking at your profile. You have no way to prioritise your follow-up outreach.

ProfileTap's creator analytics change this. Every NFC tap is a tracked profile view. You can see which contacts from the event visited your profile in the days that followed, which links they clicked — your media kit, your social channels, your collaboration inquiry form — and who came back for a second look.
