## What Is an NFC Business Card and How Does It Work?

NFC stands for Near Field Communication — a short-range wireless technology built into almost every smartphone made in the last eight years. When you tap an NFC card against the back of a phone, the phone reads the card and responds automatically.

With a ProfileTap NFC business card, that response is your full digital profile opening in the recipient's browser. Your name, designation, company, contact number, links, and any other details you have added — all visible in seconds. The person you tapped does not need to download any app, create an account, or do anything beyond letting the tap happen.

This is meaningfully different from a paper card. A paper card requires the recipient to manually save your number or, in most cases, remember to do it later (they usually don't). An NFC tap opens your profile right now, on their phone, while you are standing in front of them.

ProfileTap's NFC business cards are part of its broader [business identity management platform](/business-identity) — which means behind every tap is a live profile, built-in analytics, and a full suite of professional identity tools.

## NFC Business Card FAQs

### Do NFC business cards work on iPhones in India?

Yes. iPhones from the iPhone 7 (released 2016) onwards support NFC. When you tap a ProfileTap NFC card against the back of an iPhone running iOS 13 or later, the profile opens automatically in Safari — no action required beyond the tap. For older iPhones (6s and earlier), QR code sharing achieves the same result.

### Does the person receiving my NFC card need to install an app?

No. ProfileTap profiles open in the recipient's browser. The NFC tap triggers a URL, and the profile page loads like any website. The recipient does not need to download ProfileTap or create an account to view and save your details.

### What happens if I change my contact details after the NFC card is printed?

Nothing about the physical card needs to change. Your NFC card stores a link to your ProfileTap profile, not your contact details directly. Update your profile and the change is reflected immediately — your existing NFC card continues to work and now points to your updated information.

### Can I use one NFC card for multiple profiles?

A single NFC card is linked to one profile by default. If your account includes multi-type profiles — for example, a primary business profile and a secondary personal professional profile — you can configure which one your NFC card points to, or switch it at any time through your dashboard.

### Are ProfileTap NFC cards compatible with all Android phones?

NFC is supported on all Android phones running Android 4.4 (KitKat) or later with NFC hardware — which covers nearly all Android devices sold in India since 2014. On Android, the profile opens in Chrome or the default browser automatically on tap. Some budget Android devices do not include NFC hardware; in those cases, QR code sharing works as a fallback.
