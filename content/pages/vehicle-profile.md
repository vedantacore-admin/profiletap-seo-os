## The Wrong-Parking Problem in India

Wrong parking is one of the most common friction points of urban Indian life. Housing societies with not enough space for everyone's cars. Markets in Chennai where double-parking is routine. Office complexes in Gurugram where visitors park wherever they can find room. The standard workaround — leaving your phone number on a paper slip on the dashboard — has obvious problems. The paper blows away. The number is exposed to everyone who walks past. You get calls from strangers at all hours for situations that are not even yours.

A ProfileTap vehicle QR sticker solves the legitimate problem — the person who genuinely needs you to move your car — while protecting you from the noise and privacy risks of leaving your real number visible in public.

The sticker goes on your windscreen or rear window. Someone who needs to reach you scans it. They reach you through call privacy — no personal number exposed, no need to shout across a parking lot. You move your car. Everyone continues with their day.

**What is a vehicle QR sticker?**
A vehicle QR sticker is a printed QR code attached to your car or bike that links to your ProfileTap vehicle profile. Anyone who needs to reach you — a person whose car you have blocked, a first responder at an accident, a parking attendant — scans it and can contact you immediately. **How does call privacy work for vehicle profiles?**
When someone scans your vehicle QR and chooses to call you, the call routes through ProfileTap's call masking system. You receive the call on your real phone number. The caller hears it ringing and reaches you normally. Neither party ever sees the other's personal phone number during the interaction. The privacy is complete on both sides.

Yes. ProfileTap's multi-type profiles feature lets you create separate vehicle profiles for each vehicle you own — a car, a bike, a second family vehicle — all managed from the same account. Each profile has its own QR code. You print and attach each one to the relevant vehicle.

At minimum: your name and a contact option (handled through call privacy). You control exactly what is visible. Keep it short and clear — the person scanning it may be in a hurry.
