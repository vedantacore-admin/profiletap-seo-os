## What to Look for in a Link in Bio Tool

Not all link in bio tools are equal. Before comparing options, here are the five criteria that matter most for creators based in India.

**1. Custom Domain Support**
Your link in your bio should use your own brand name — not `linktr.ee/yourname`. Custom domain support signals professionalism to brands. Check whether it's available on free or only paid plans.

**2. Analytics Depth**
Basic tools show you total clicks. Good tools show you which links are clicked, from which platforms, at what time. Advanced tools show you who's clicking — so you can tell when a brand manager is scoping your profile versus a regular follower.

**3. Multi-Link Support**
The minimum requirement. Every tool on this list supports multiple links. What varies is how flexible the layout is — can you group links, add thumbnails, embed video, or add social icons cleanly?

**4. India Pricing**
Most link in bio tools price in USD, which adds up. A $10/month tool becomes ₹840+ per month. Check whether the tool has India-based pricing, UPI/RuPay support, or a free plan that covers your actual needs.

**5. Multi-Profile or Multi-Type Support**
If you're a creator who also does freelance work, or has both a personal brand and a business identity, you need a tool that supports more than one profile type — not just stacked links.

## Tool-by-Tool Breakdown

### Linktree

Linktree is the default choice for most creators simply because it was first. It does what it says: stacks your links in a clean page. The free plan works fine for basic use — multiple links, simple design options, thumbnail support.

The limitations show up fast. Analytics on the free plan are extremely thin — you get total views, that's it. Custom domain requires a paid plan (Starter at $5/month USD, Pro at $9/month). If you're running brand deals and need to prove your link traffic to a sponsor, Linktree free won't cut it. The Pro plan's analytics are genuinely useful, but you're paying $108/year in USD — roughly ₹9,000 at current rates — for a link page that still doesn't support multiple profile types.

For Indian creators just getting started, it's fine. For anyone taking brand work seriously, it starts to feel like paying for a basic tool.

### Beacons

Beacons is the most feature-rich tool in this comparison. Beyond links, it lets you add a store (sell digital products, 1:1 bookings, brand kit), an email capture form, and embedded media. The free plan is generous — it includes a custom domain, which is unusually good for a free tier.

Analytics on Beacons go further than Linktree free: you can see traffic sources and click data by link. The paid plans unlock audience demographics and integration with brand deals marketplace.

The downside for Indian creators: pricing is in USD (Creator plan $10/month, Business $30/month), there's no UPI or INR payment option, and the platform is clearly built for US-market brand deals. If you work primarily with Indian brands and agencies, the brand marketplace won't help you much.

Still, for a feature-rich free tier, Beacons is hard to beat internationally.

### Bio.link

Bio.link is a minimalist tool — clean design, fast setup, multiple links, no clutter. The free plan is fully functional for basic linking. It's popular with creators who want a simple, stylish page without fuss.

Analytics are basic on free (view count, click count), and custom domains require the paid plan. There's no multi-profile support, no advanced creator analytics, no offline sharing functionality.

Bio.link suits creators who want a clean page and don't need data depth. It's not the right choice if you're actively managing brand partnerships or want to understand who's visiting your profile.

### Milkshake

Milkshake is a mobile-first link in bio tool with a card-style layout that looks different from the standard stacked list. You build your page directly from the app on your phone — no desktop required. Design options are nice.

The analytics are minimal (page views only), there's no custom domain support, and the feature set hasn't expanded significantly since launch. It works for creators who prioritise aesthetics over data and don't need brand-facing functionality.

For Indian creators managing active brand deals or growing a professional identity, Milkshake is too light.

To unlock click-level analytics and custom domains, you need Linktree Starter ($5/month USD) or Pro ($9/month USD), billed in USD only.

### Which link in bio tool has the best analytics?

Technically yes, but it creates confusion. If your Instagram bio link goes to Linktree and your YouTube description points to a different tool, your audience and brands get a fragmented experience. The better approach: pick one hub, build it properly, and use it consistently everywhere.

Beacons has more built-in features (digital product sales, booking forms) if those are relevant to your work.
