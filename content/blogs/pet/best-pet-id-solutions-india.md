# Best Pet ID Solutions in India: Tags, QR Codes, and Smart Options (2026)

Losing a pet is one of the most stressful experiences a pet owner can go through. You post in every WhatsApp group, you call nearby vets, you walk the streets calling your dog's name. The right ID tag won't guarantee your pet never goes missing — but it is the single most effective tool for getting them home when they do.

## Types of Pet ID Solutions Available in India

### 1. Engraved Metal Tags

The traditional option. A small metal tag — usually stainless steel, brass, or aluminium — with your pet's name and your phone number engraved directly onto it. They're inexpensive, durable, and widely available across India.

The limitation is obvious: once engraved, the information is fixed. If your number changes, the tag is wrong. If you move cities, the tag is outdated. Information capacity is limited to what fits on a small disc.

### 2. Silicone Tags

Soft, lightweight, and quiet — silicone tags don't make the jingle-jangle noise of metal tags, which many pet owners (and neighbours) appreciate. They're printed rather than engraved, which means slightly more text can fit. They're less durable than metal over the long term.

Good for cats and small dogs where a heavy metal tag would be uncomfortable.

### 3. Embroidered Collars

Some collars have the owner's name and number stitched directly into the fabric. These are comfortable and impossible to lose (the ID is part of the collar), but they share the same core problem as engraved tags: the information is fixed.

### 4. GPS Trackers

GPS pet trackers attach to a collar and let you see your pet's real-time location on your phone. Brands like Tractive and various India-market options are available. They're the most powerful tool for finding a lost pet — but they come with meaningful trade-offs.

They require a SIM card and a monthly subscription. The battery needs charging regularly (some every 2-3 days). They add weight to the collar. And the tracker only helps if it's charged, active, and still on the collar when your pet goes missing.

GPS trackers are genuinely useful — especially for dogs that escape frequently — but they're a complement to, not a replacement for, basic ID.

### 5. QR Smart Tags

A QR code tag links to a digital pet profile that you can update any time. When someone finds your pet and scans the code, they see your pet's photo, name, breed, your contact number, vet information, and medical notes — all in one screen, no app required.

The profile behind the code can be updated in seconds from your phone. Change your number, add a new medical note, update your address — the physical tag on your pet's collar never needs replacing.

## Where to Buy Pet ID Tags in India

**Online marketplaces:**
Amazon.in and Flipkart both carry a wide range of engraved and silicone tags. Search for "dog ID tag" or "pet name tag" and you'll find options ranging from ₹80 to ₹500. Delivery is available across most of India. Quality varies — read reviews carefully, especially for tags that claim to be laser engraved.

**Offline pet stores:**
In major cities, most pet stores will either stock engraved tags or offer in-store engraving. In Mumbai, stores in areas like Bandra, Juhu, and Powai; in Delhi, markets in Lajpat Nagar and South Ex; in Bangalore, stores around Indiranagar and Koramangala typically carry pet ID options. Call ahead to confirm engraving services.

**Veterinary clinics:**
Many vet clinics sell basic ID tags and some offer engraving services. This is a convenient option when you're already bringing your pet in for vaccination.

## Why QR Smart Tags Are the Future of Pet ID in India

India's smartphone penetration has changed the math on QR codes dramatically. Virtually every smartphone sold in India in the last five years can scan a QR code directly from the camera app — no separate app, no confusion.

When a stranger finds a lost dog in a Mumbai lane or a housing society in Gurgaon, they're almost certainly carrying a smartphone. Scanning a QR code is a natural, familiar action in a country where QR-based UPI payments are ubiquitous.

**Always up to date.** This is the biggest advantage. You're not relying on information engraved months or years ago. Your current number, your current address, your pet's current medical status — all available in real time.

**Stores far more than a name and number.** A finder who sees a dog with a medical condition label ("diabetic — needs insulin every 12 hours") is equipped to make better decisions about the animal's immediate care, especially if they take it to a vet.

**Works with any smartphone.** Unlike GPS tracking apps that require the finder to download something, a QR tag opens a webpage. No friction, no app store, no barrier.

**Privacy protection built in.** ProfileTap includes call masking — a stranger scanning your pet's tag can call or WhatsApp you without seeing your permanent personal number. This matters when your pet's profile might be scanned by dozens of strangers during a search.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much do pet ID tags cost in India?

Basic engraved tags cost ₹50–₹300 on Amazon.in or Flipkart. Custom silicone tags run ₹80–₹400. GPS trackers are ₹2,000–₹8,000 with ongoing subscription fees. For a QR smart tag, the cost is the price of the physical tag (₹100–₹400 for a printable or ordered tag) plus a ProfileTap account — making it very cost-competitive, especially since you never need to buy a replacement tag when your details change.

### Where can I get a pet ID tag engraved in India?

Most pet stores in major cities offer engraving, either in-store or via order. Online, Amazon.in sellers like "PawPrint" and similar pet accessory brands offer personalised engraved tags with 3–5 day delivery. You can also find local engravers (the same ones who make keychains) in markets in most Indian cities — they typically charge ₹100–₹200.

### Can I add medical information to my pet's ID?

With a traditional engraved tag, you can add one short line — "DIABETIC" or "NEEDS MEDS" — if space allows. With a QR smart tag, there's no practical limit. You can add full medication schedules, allergy details, vet contact, vaccination history, and behavioural notes. This information can be a genuine help to a vet who takes in a found animal.

### What happens if the finder doesn't have a smartphone?

This is the main limitation of QR tags. If the person who finds your pet doesn't have a smartphone, they can't scan the code. This is why a combination approach works best: an engraved tag with your current phone number alongside a QR tag with your full profile. The engraved tag handles the non-smartphone scenario; the QR tag handles everything else. India's smartphone penetration in urban areas is now high enough that the QR tag will work in the vast majority of cases.

## Key Takeaways

India has more pet ID options than ever — from cheap engraved tags to GPS trackers — but QR smart tags occupy a unique position: they combine low cost with high information capacity and the crucial advantage of always being up to date. The best setup for most Indian pet owners is an engraved tag as a backup plus a QR smart profile as the primary contact tool, giving any finder multiple paths to reach you.
