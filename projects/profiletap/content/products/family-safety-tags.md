# Family Safety Tags with QR Code — Emergency Identification for Children and Elderly

A family safety tag with a QR code provides immediate access to emergency contacts and critical medical information when the person carrying it cannot communicate for themselves. A child separated from parents in a crowd. An elderly parent who becomes disoriented. A family member with a medical condition who collapses in a public place. In each of these situations, the person who finds them needs information fast — and a ProfileTap family safety tag delivers it in a single scan.

## Why Family Safety Tags Exist

Traditional identification methods fail in emergencies. A card in a wallet may not be found. A phone may be locked. A child may be too frightened to speak. An elderly person with dementia may not remember their name or address.

A QR safety tag worn visibly on the body — on a wrist, a bag, a belt, a keychain — is designed to be found and scanned by whoever is nearby. It does not require the wearer to do anything. It does not require the finder to have a specific app. It requires only a smartphone camera and two seconds of attention.

The profile that opens contains exactly what the finder needs: the wearer's name, emergency contacts, medical conditions, allergies, medications, and any instructions relevant to the situation. The finder calls the emergency contact through ProfileTap's call masking system. Help arrives. The outcome is better because the right information was available at the right moment.

## How It Works in an Emergency

The value of a family safety tag is measured in the minutes after something goes wrong.

**Scenario: A child is separated at a railway station.**
A five-year-old gets off the train at the wrong stop. A co-passenger notices the child is alone and frightened. They see the QR tag on the child's school bag. They scan it. The child's profile opens — name, parents' contact numbers, home address, and a photo. They tap the call button. The call routes through call masking — the parent's phone rings. The parent answers, confirms the situation, and coordinates with railway staff. The child is reunited within the hour.

**Scenario: An elderly parent collapses at a market.**
A 72-year-old with a heart condition becomes unwell while buying vegetables. A shopkeeper notices the QR tag clipped to their belt. They scan it. The profile shows the person's name, their daughter's phone number, their cardiologist's name, current medications, and a note: "If unresponsive, call the emergency contact immediately — do not wait." The shopkeeper calls. The daughter answers. An ambulance is directed. The attending doctor has the medication list before the patient arrives.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What information should I include in a safety profile?
At minimum: the wearer's name, a recent photo, and at least one emergency contact number. For elderly family members or individuals with medical conditions, include blood type, current medications, known allergies, the treating doctor's name and number, and any instructions relevant to a medical emergency (for example, "diabetic — if found unresponsive, check blood sugar"). You control exactly what is visible.

### Is call masking appropriate for emergency situations — will it slow things down?
No. Call masking adds no delay to the call. The finder taps the call button, the call routes through ProfileTap's system in real time, and your phone rings immediately. The experience for both parties is identical to a normal phone call. The only difference is that neither party's personal number is visible to the other.

### Can I create safety profiles for multiple family members?
Yes. ProfileTap supports multiple profiles under one account. Create a safety profile for each child, elderly parent, or family member who needs one. Each profile has its own QR code and its own tag. You manage and update all profiles from a single dashboard.

### How do I keep the safety profile information current?
The QR code on the tag always links to the same profile URL. When medications change, when you move to a new city, when a child starts a new school — update the profile online and the next scan reflects the current information. You do not need to replace the tag when details change. Set a calendar reminder to review the profile quarterly, or after any significant medical or contact change.
