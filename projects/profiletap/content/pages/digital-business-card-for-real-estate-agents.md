# Digital Business Card for Real Estate Agents: Close More Deals, Lose Fewer Leads

Real estate is a contact sport. You meet dozens of buyers and sellers every week — at site visits, builder expos, project launches, and casual referrals. You hand out paper cards at every one of those touchpoints. Most of those cards are in someone's pocket right now, or at the bottom of a bag, or in the bin outside a project office on the outskirts of Thane.

The leads you worked hardest to meet are the ones most likely to lose your details. ProfileTap's digital business card for real estate agents changes how you share, track, and follow up — from the first site visit to deal closure.

## The Real Problems Property Agents Face

### Dozens of site visits, hundreds of lost leads

A busy property agent in Mumbai or Pune can do fifteen to twenty site visits in a week. Every visit involves sharing contact details with prospective buyers. Some of those buyers are serious. Most are browsing. All of them need your number before you can convert them.

Paper cards solve the exchange problem but not the follow-up problem. You have no way of knowing which buyers from last Tuesday's site visit looked you up again, visited your profile, or compared you to a competitor. You are flying blind.

### Your personal number in the hands of strangers

Real estate means sharing your mobile number with people you have just met — buyers, renters, landowners, builders. Most of them are professional contacts. Some will misuse that accessibility. Your personal number stays in the contacts of hundreds of people you may never convert.

Call masking is not a luxury for property agents — it is basic professional hygiene.

### No systematic way to collect reviews after deal closure

Closing a property deal is one of the most significant financial events in a buyer's life. Satisfied buyers are often genuinely willing to leave a review. But asking for one in the moment of handover feels transactional, and following up later is easy to forget. Most agents leave a trail of happy clients who never became public advocates.

### Brokerage teams look inconsistent

A brokerage firm with twelve agents often has twelve different ways of presenting itself. Some agents have paper cards with the firm logo, some have typed-out templates, some share a screenshot of their WhatsApp contact. To a buyer comparing options, inconsistency signals disorganisation. It costs deals.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does the buyer need to download an app to receive my profile?**
No. When a buyer taps your NFC card or scans your QR code, their phone's browser opens your ProfileTap profile directly. There is nothing to install. It works on every iPhone and Android device without any prior setup.

**Can I track which buyers from a site visit actually engaged with my profile?**
Yes. ProfileTap's analytics record a profile view for every NFC tap or QR scan. You can see total views, link clicks, and return visits. While the analytics do not capture individual buyer identities by default, you can cross-reference timing data with your own records from a specific site visit or event.

**How does call masking work for real estate agents?**
When call masking is enabled on your profile, buyers see a masked call link rather than your personal mobile number. When they use that link to call, the call routes to your actual phone through the platform's privacy layer. Your personal number is never revealed. You can update your masking settings at any time — for example, disabling it temporarily for a specific project or period.

**Can I manage profiles for my entire brokerage team from one account?**
Yes. ProfileTap's manage multiple accounts / teams feature is designed for exactly this use case. A brokerage principal or admin can create and manage individual profiles for every agent on the team, control branding consistency, and review analytics across all agents from a single dashboard.

**What happens to my QR codes and NFC taps if I change my agency or phone number?**
Your ProfileTap profile is a live document. When you update your contact details, agency name, or any other information, the change is reflected immediately everywhere your profile has been shared — including all past NFC taps and QR scans. You do not need to reprint materials or redistribute your details. Every buyer who has previously scanned your code will see the updated information the next time they open the link.
